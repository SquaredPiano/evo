CFD (Cutting Frequency Determination) scoring tables
=====================================================

Source
------
Doench, J.G., Fusi, N., Sullender, M. et al.
"Optimized sgRNA design to maximize activity and minimize off-target
effects of CRISPR-Cas9." Nature Biotechnology 34, 184-191 (2016).
https://doi.org/10.1038/nbt.3437

The two tables below are the mismatch and PAM weight lookups distributed
with the Doench 2016 CFD calculator (Supplementary Table 19), redistributed
here in JSON form (unpickled from the reference `mismatch_score.pkl` and
`pam_scores.pkl`).

cfd_mismatch_scores.json
------------------------
240 entries. Key format: "r<GUIDE_RNA_BASE>:d<TARGET_DNA_BASE>,<POSITION>".
The RNA base is the guide (spacer) base; the DNA base is the base on the
target (protospacer-complementary) strand. POSITION is 1..20 with 20 being
the PAM-proximal end. The value is the retained cutting fraction (0..1) for
that single rNA:dDNA mismatch at that position. Watson-Crick matches are not
stored (they contribute a factor of 1.0).

cfd_pam_scores.json
-------------------
16 entries keyed by the 2 nt immediately 3' of the protospacer (positions 2-3
of an NGG PAM). Value is the retained cutting fraction for that PAM. "GG" = 1.0.

MIT (Hsu 2013) weights are defined inline in services/crispr_offtarget.py:
Hsu, P.D., Scott, D.A., Weinstein, J.A. et al. "DNA targeting specificity of
RNA-guided Cas9 nucleases." Nature Biotechnology 31, 827-832 (2013).
